Frequently Asked Questions
What are the most important to know about riding a bike?
Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.
How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?
Helmets should be replaced every five years, or immediately after a crash. Check the manufacturer's date label inside your helmet to determine its age.
My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?
Before riding a bike that has been in storage, check the tires for proper inflation, inspect the brakes, and ensure that all fluids (oil, coolant, brake fluid) are at the correct levels. It's also a good idea to test ride the bike in a safe area to ensure everything is functioning properly.
What rules should I follow when riding my bike?
Always wear a helmet and protective gear. Follow traffic laws and signals. Stay visible to other drivers by using lights and reflective clothing. Maintain a safe distance from other vehicles and be cautious at intersections.
